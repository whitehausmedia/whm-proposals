Before we talk numbers

A few ideas worth understanding first.

The proposal makes more sense once these three concepts are clear. We've kept them short on purpose.

SEO is compound interest for traffic.

Paid ads stop the moment you stop paying. SEO keeps delivering. When someone searches "Black-owned luxury perfume Atlanta" or "gourmand perfume gift set," the goal is for ByLeCrème to be the answer. Every product page, blog post, and review we publish makes the brand more discoverable. That visibility compounds over months and years. It's the difference between renting traffic and owning it.

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews are now part of the discovery layer.

When a buyer asks an AI assistant for a Black-owned luxury fragrance brand based in Atlanta, the answer is determined by what's on the site, in the structured data, and in the reviews. Almost no fragrance brand is optimizing for this yet. That's an open lane. ByLeCrème's existing AI Concierge is already ahead of the curve. We make sure the brand shows up in AI-generated answers across every platform that matters.

Meta leads. Google captures intent. TikTok scales with content.

ByLeCrème is visual, emotional, and experience-driven. That's a perfect fit for Meta, which gets 60 percent of the paid media weighting. Google takes 25 percent for high-intent searches. TikTok takes the remaining 15 percent and scales up only when short-form content output is consistent. The split reflects where this brand has the strongest fit, not where every brand should spend.
