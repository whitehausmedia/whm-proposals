Where the opportunity lives

Four things holding your online presence back.

After reviewing your current site and how visitors experience it, here's what we found — and why it's costing you members.

01

No Class Schedule or Booking Path Online

If someone wants to take a class at Elite Core Studios, there's no clear way to do it from your website. No schedule, no booking integration, no trial class CTA. Every interested visitor has to hunt for your contact info and hope someone responds. In boutique fitness, that friction kills conversion — people book where it's easiest.

02

The Lagree Method Isn't Explained

The Megaformer is a unique piece of equipment and Lagree is a unique method — but your site assumes visitors already know what both mean. Most don't. Without a clear "What is Lagree?" section explaining the high-intensity, low-impact approach and why it delivers results faster than traditional Pilates or HIIT, you lose people before they're even curious.

03

No Instructor Profiles or Faces

Boutique fitness is deeply personal. People don't just join a studio — they join for specific instructors. When there are no photos, bios, or credentials for your trainers, you're removing one of the most powerful trust signals in this industry. Visitors want to know who's going to be pushing them through that 50-minute session.

04

Missing Social Proof and Member Results

Lagree gets results — real, visible, measurable results. But there's nothing on your site to show that. No testimonials, no member journeys, no before-and-after context. In a world where potential members are comparing studios online before ever setting foot inside, social proof is the difference between "I'll check it out" and "I'm booking a trial right now."
