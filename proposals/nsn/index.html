The Honest Assessment

The Organization Has Outgrown
Its Digital Presence.

We'll say this the way you'd say it to a nonprofit client: the infrastructure hasn't kept pace with the organization. That's not a criticism — it's the most important growth opportunity on the table.

David advises nonprofits on how to scale revenue while holding expenses down. The principle he brings to those engagements applies here too: when your digital platform isn't working for you, you're working harder than you should have to.

Right now, NSN's website is a scheduling brochure for conferences. It tells people events are happening and invites them to join. It does not demonstrate value. It does not retain members between events. It does not give the 70+ sponsor partners a year-round commercial surface. And it doesn't yet give the 100,000+ people in the network a reason to engage digitally when there's no conference on the calendar.

This isn't about aesthetics. A redesigned digital platform is a revenue decision. It changes how many people join, how many renew, how many sponsors commit at higher tiers, and how many of the 100K+ contacts in the network become paying members. The website is currently the weakest link between NSN's mission and its ability to fund it.

03
The Vision

Three Pillars. One Platform.

The new NSN digital experience is built around three core pillars — each one tied directly to a revenue outcome and a member outcome. Together, they turn the website from a brochure into the operational backbone of the organization.

Pillar 01
The Member Experience
A fully realized member hub that gives professionals a reason to visit, engage, and stay — 365 days a year, not just during conference season.
  • Gated member portal — on-brand, on-domain, not a third-party redirect
  • Resource library — on-demand sales training, career tools, exclusive content
  • Member directory — searchable network of 100K+ professionals
  • Chapter events calendar — all 20 chapters, unified in one place
  • Member spotlight hub — the chapter-level stories that currently only live on Instagram, now amplified on the website
  • Tiered membership — entry-level, professional, and executive tiers that reduce friction to join and increase lifetime value
Pillar 02
The Sponsor Engine
A year-round commercial platform that gives corporate partners ongoing value — not just a conference weekend. The site becomes a revenue surface that runs independently of the event calendar.
  • Live sponsor hub — transparent tiers, ROI data, real audience demographics
  • Self-serve packages — lower-tier sponsors can select, pay, and activate without a sales call
  • Digital ad inventory — newsletter sponsorships, homepage placements, content series branding
  • Job board — employers post directly to the NSN member base; hiring outcomes tracked and reported
  • Partner case studies — how Salesforce, Microsoft, and J&J have used NSN to diversify their sales organizations
Pillar 03
The Growth Infrastructure
The backend systems that make the entire organization easier to scale — new chapters, new markets, new members — without adding operational overhead.
  • Chapter page system — 20 templated, SEO-optimized local landing pages; new chapters deploy in hours
  • Local search capture — "sales networking events in [city]" routes to the NSN chapter page in that market
  • Conference bridge — seamless path from conference registration to membership, no drop-off
  • Sales Influencer program integration — the referral program gets a dedicated acquisition funnel
  • 10+ years of media surfaced — conference videos, photos, testimonials become homepage conversion assets
