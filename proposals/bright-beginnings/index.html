What we noticed

A few things stood out when we looked at your site.

None of these are criticisms — your business speaks for itself. These are just places where the website is not keeping up with the reputation you have earned.

01

The photos do not show your center

When I clicked through the site, I noticed the images are mostly stock photography — they could be any daycare, anywhere. None of them show your actual classrooms, your playground, or the people who work there every day. Parents browsing your site are trying to picture their child in your space, and right now the site does not give them that.

02

The mobile experience needs work

I pulled up the site on my phone, and it is tough to navigate. Text is small, buttons are hard to tap, and the layout does not adjust well to a smaller screen. That matters because most parents searching for childcare in Cary are doing it from their phone — and if the site is hard to use, they are going to move on before they ever learn what makes Bright Beginnings different.

03

Your family story deserves a spotlight

This is the thing that really stood out to me. You and Leon did not just open a daycare — you built this on family land, after long careers as educators, and now your children are part of the leadership. That story is what separates Bright Beginnings from every KinderCare and franchise in the area. But if a parent only visits your website, they would never know any of it. That story should be front and center.

04

Families searching online may not find you

When someone in Cary searches for "daycare near me" or "child care in Cary," Google decides who shows up first based on how well a website is structured behind the scenes. Right now, the site is missing some of the basics that help with that — things like proper page titles, location details, and the technical tags that tell Google what your business is. Fixing this would help more local families discover you.

What we would build
