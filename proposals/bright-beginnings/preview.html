Our Story

Built by educators. Run by family.

Bright Beginnings was founded in May 2000 by Shirley and Leon Herndon — both retired educators with over 70 combined years in the Wake County and Chatham County school systems. Shirley spent 32 years as a teacher and assistant principal. Leon served 38 years as a teacher, assistant principal, and principal.

Their philosophy is simple: the children who thrive in school are the ones who were lovingly taught, encouraged, and challenged early. Today, their daughter Lisa Ware serves as Assistant Director and their son Linwood as Marketing Director. Every grandchild in the family has attended the program.

Our Approach

The Creative Curriculum® System

Young children learn best by doing. Our curriculum is built on the belief that learning is not just repeating what someone else says — it requires active thinking, hands-on exploration, and purposeful play.

♥

Social-Emotional Growth

Building confidence, empathy, and the social skills children need to thrive in group settings and beyond.

★

Cognitive Development

Problem-solving, critical thinking, and early math and science concepts through guided exploration.

✎

Language & Literacy

Rich vocabulary, early reading skills, and expressive communication through stories, songs, and conversation.

♪

Physical Development

Fine and gross motor skills through art, outdoor play, music, and hands-on activities.

Programs

Care designed for every stage

From 3 months through kindergarten readiness, each program is built around where your child is developmentally — and where they are headed next.

3 - 12 Months

Infant Care

👶 1:4 Ratio

Nurturing, individualized attention in a warm and secure environment. Each baby follows their own schedule for feeding, napping, and sensory play with a dedicated caregiver they know and trust.

12 - 24 Months

Toddler Program

👧 1:5 Ratio

Hands-on exploration and early social development. Language-rich activities that spark curiosity and build the confidence toddlers need to engage with the world around them.

24 - 36 Months

Preschool I

📖 1:8 Ratio

Structured play and early academics in a nurturing classroom setting.

3 Years Old

Preschool II

🎨 1:9 Ratio

Creative Curriculum in action — building kindergarten readiness through purposeful play and guided learning.

4 - 5 Years Old

Kinderprep

🎓 1:12 Ratio

Advanced preparation for kindergarten with structured literacy, math readiness, and social skills development.

Why Bright Beginnings

What families value most

01

Founded by Educators, Not Investors

Shirley and Leon Herndon built this center after 70 combined years in public education. Every decision — from curriculum to hiring — is informed by what actually helps children learn.

02

Below-Required Ratios

Our student-to-teacher ratios are lower than what the state requires. That means more individual attention, more meaningful interactions, and fewer children competing for a teacher's time.

03

26 Years of Community Trust

Since May 2000, Cary families have trusted Bright Beginnings with what matters most. That kind of longevity speaks to the consistency of care we provide every single day.

04

Low Staff Turnover

Many of our teachers have been here for years. Children build real relationships with their caregivers — the kind of consistency that creates security and accelerates development.

05

Diversity as a Strength

Our diverse student body and staff reflect the world outside. This reinforces to every child that "the world looks like me" — a value the Herndons have championed since day one.

06

Smartcare Parent App

Stay connected throughout the day with real-time updates, daily activity reports, and progress tracking through the Smartcare platform. Two parent-teacher conferences annually.
