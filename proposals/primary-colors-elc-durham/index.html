Where the opportunity lives

Four gaps costing you enrollments every month.

After reviewing your current website and digital presence, we identified four specific areas where your online presence is working against thirty years of earned reputation.

01

Zero Social Media Presence

Your website has no links to Facebook, Instagram, or any social platform — and your website itself has no social presence either. In 2026, parents research childcare on Instagram before they ever call. When they search "Primary Colors Durham" on social and find nothing, they assume the center is either closed or not actively caring for children currently. Competitors with even a basic Instagram are winning those parents.

02

Outdated Design Undermines Your Credentials

The current site uses a mid-2010s layout — minimal imagery, small fonts, generic styling. You hold a 5-star NC rating and NAEYC accreditation. Those credentials should be front and center, displayed with the weight they deserve. Instead, they're buried in text that parents skim past. A parent comparing your site to a competitor's modern, photo-rich website will make a snap judgment about your facility's quality before ever calling.

03

No Enrollment Path for Ready Parents

The only action a parent can take on your site is submit a generic "How can we help you?" form. There's no "Schedule a Tour" button, no "Check Availability," no enrollment inquiry flow. When a family decides they're interested in Primary Colors — often at 10pm while researching on their phone — there's no clear next step. That friction is the difference between an inquiry and a lost enrollment.

04

Your 5-Star Reputation Is Hidden

Parents who find your Google listing see strong reviews. Parents who land on your website see none of that. There's no testimonial section, no review widget, no social proof of any kind. A site that shows 20+ happy parents saying "this place changed our family" closes enrollments. Right now, parents have to leave your site and go find the reviews themselves — and many don't.
