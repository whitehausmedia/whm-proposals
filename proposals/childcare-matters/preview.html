Our Story

A community built on trust since 1983

Childcare Matters began at the Church of the Holy Family in 1983 and has been a cornerstone of the Chapel Hill community ever since. Now located at 825 North Estes Drive, we are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit governed by a parent-led board of directors — meaning every decision centers on what is best for children and families.

Our director, Ms. Alesha, has spent 25 years at this center. Our teachers stay for years and decades, not semesters. That continuity creates relationships children can depend on — and it is what sets us apart from every other option in the Triangle.

Our Classrooms

Five rooms, five worlds of discovery

Each classroom is designed around where your child is developmentally — small groups, dedicated teachers, and a philosophy rooted in learning through play.

6 Weeks - 1 Year

Dreamers

One-on-one attention in a nurturing environment. Individualized schedules for feeding, napping, and play. Songs, sounds, and textures that spark early discovery with Ms. Maritza and Ms. Kathy.

1 - 2 Years

Moonbeams

Center play, singing, and hands-on learning with an emphasis on art and multi-step directions. Ms. Lisa guides your toddler through the wonder of early independence.

2 - 3.5 Years

Stars

Art, music, science, reading, blocks, and field trips. Self-expression takes center stage with Ms. Latesha and Ms. Angela.

3 - 4 Years

Super Stars

Language development, fine motor skills, and social-emotional learning through interactive play. Ms. Sasha helps every child find their voice.

4 - 6 Years

Rockets

Kindergarten readiness with independent thinking and problem-solving at the core. Ms. Anaejah and Ms. Julia prepare your child for the next chapter.

Why Childcare Matters

What Chapel Hill families value most

01

Nonprofit, Community-Owned

We are not a franchise or a corporate chain. Childcare Matters is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit run by a parent-led board. Every dollar goes back into the care your child receives.

02

Teachers Who Stay

Our staff tenure is measured in years and decades, not months. That stability means your child builds real relationships with caregivers who know them deeply.

03

Small by Design

With just 47 children across five classrooms, every child is seen, heard, and known. Small ratios mean more individual attention at every developmental stage.

04

Accessible to Every Family

Sliding scale tuition, over $100,000 in annual subsidies, and discounts for UNC and Duke employees. Quality early childhood education should not depend on income.

05

Four Decades of Trust

Since 1983, Chapel Hill families have chosen Childcare Matters. Award-winning care recognized by Chapel Hill News and IndyWeek speaks to the relationships built here every day.

06

Learning Through Play

Our philosophy centers on developmentally appropriate practice — art, music, science, field trips, and hands-on exploration. Children learn best when they are curious, comfortable, and cared for.
